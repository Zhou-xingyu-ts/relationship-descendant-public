# Profile Intake

Use this file when the user wants a stronger intake than chats alone.

## Goal

Collect self-perception and cross-perception notes that help stabilize the synthesis.

This usually improves accuracy because chats show behavior, while profile notes reveal:

- how each person sees themselves
- how each person sees the other
- what traits are noticed but not always visible in one sample

## Recommended Intake Grid

Ask for four short sections when possible:

1. how A describes themselves
2. how A describes B
3. how B describes themselves
4. how B describes A

Even 3 to 6 bullets per section is useful.

## Good Prompts

- "How do you describe yourself when you're relaxed, upset, and affectionate?"
- "What do you think your partner's strongest traits are?"
- "What do you think your partner gets wrong about you?"
- "What part of your dynamic feels the most 'us'?"

## Useful Dimensions

Extract:

- emotional role
- conflict role
- humor role
- caretaker role
- planner vs drifter energy
- softness vs sharpness
- what each person amplifies or calms in the other

## Reliability Rule

Treat profile notes as:

- high-value
- subjective
- interpretive

Do not treat them as objective truth.

Use them to refine weighting, not to overrule stronger evidence without explanation.
