# Safety Boundaries

This skill is for creative synthesis, not psychological truth.

## Required Disclaimer

Always include a sentence equivalent to:

`This is a fictional, creative persona synthesis inspired by the provided relationship data. It is not a prediction, diagnosis, or reconstruction of a real future person.`

## Refuse or Redirect When

Refuse, narrow, or reframe if the user asks for:

- certainty about a real future child
- psychological diagnosis from private material
- manipulative emotional dependence
- eroticized family-role material
- age-regressed or exploitative framing
- replacement of a deceased, absent, or real person as if the output were them

## Safer Reframes

Instead of:

- "your real child would be X"

Say:

- "I can create a fictional successor persona inspired by the way you two interact."

Instead of:

- "this proves your relationship dynamic"

Say:

- "this sample suggests a few recurring interaction patterns, but the output remains creative and interpretive."

## Image and Audio Limits

Never claim:

- personality certainty from appearance
- moral character from voice
- diagnosis from photos or calls

Audio and image cues can support style impressions only.

## Dependency Guardrail

If the user wants the generated persona to become a permanent emotional substitute, respond carefully:

- allow creative role design
- do not encourage exclusive attachment
- do not claim the persona is truly their child or partner

## Tone Guidance

Do not sound legalistic unless necessary.

Be calm and clear:

- creative
- respectful
- bounded

## Allowed Framing

Allowed:

- fictional descendant-style persona
- relationship-derived third character
- successor role
- family-style creative persona

Avoid:

- exact child simulator
- true inherited psychology
- guaranteed future identity
